## 🎯 30-Second Pitch (pick the right version for your audience)

**Version A — For PE Operators (GPs, Partners, Principals):**
> "PE OS is the AI-powered deal operating system for private equity. Instead of copying financials from CIMs into Excel for hours, you drag the PDF in and in 30 seconds you have structured financials, a risk flag, and a chatbot that answers math questions about the deal with citations. Think of it as replacing Excel + Notion + a junior analyst for the early-stage screening work."

**Version B — For Investment Analysts / Associates:**
> "PE OS is what I wish I had when I was reviewing 50 CIMs a week. You upload a CIM, AI extracts the financials, flags risks, and you can ask it things like 'what's the EBITDA margin trend' and get real answers with formulas — not made up numbers. Your memos practically write themselves."

**Version C — For Non-Technical Stakeholders:**
> "PE OS turns your deal pipeline into an AI-powered command center. Upload any deal document and get instant financial analysis, risk flags, and a team workspace. It's built specifically for private equity workflows — deal rooms, investment memos, pipeline tracking, all in one place."

## 🔍 Discovery Questions (ask FIRST, before opening PE OS)

Take notes on screen so they see you listening.

1. **"Tell me about your firm — fund size, team, deal volume?"**
2. **"Walk me through what happens when a CIM lands in your inbox today."**
3. **"What part of that takes the most time?"**
4. **"What tools do you use right now? Excel? Notion? DealCloud? Something custom?"**
5. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your deal process, what would it be?"** ← THIS IS THE KEY QUESTION
6. **"How do you decide which deals to spend more time on?"**
7. **"Who else on your team would need this?"**

**→ Write their exact words. Use them back in the demo.**

## 🎬 Demo Script (15 minutes of actual demo)

### Opening shot — Dashboard (30 sec)
"This is what you see Monday morning. One deal flow view, one AI insights feed, one task list."
→ Point out the 3 deals at different stages, the AI-generated thesis on each card.

### Hero moment #1 — Upload a CIM & watch the magic (3 min)
1. Click into **Apex Precision** deal
2. "We pre-loaded this to save your time, but here's what happens on a real upload…"
3. Scroll down to **Financial Statements** — show 3 years P&L, BS, CF
4. "This came out of a PDF in 30 seconds. The AI agent extracts, validates, and self-corrects."
5. Show the **agent log** if they ask "how does it work"

### Hero moment #2 — Deal Chat math (THE WOW) (4 min)
1. Scroll to the chat panel on Apex
2. Type live: **"What's the EBITDA margin trend from 2023 to 2025?"**
3. **Wait for response** — don't fill the silence
4. Read the answer out loud: "Notice how it quotes exact numbers and shows the formula? That's because the LLM has the full financial table in its context, not just a vague summary."
5. Ask a comparison question: **"How does this compare to Meridian Analytics?"**
6. "Our agent has 6 tools — comparison, search, financial lookup, memo drafting, document search, and risk analysis. All grounded in YOUR data, not made-up numbers."

### Hero moment #3 — Memo Builder (3 min)
1. Click "Generate Memo" on Apex
2. Show the AI drafting sections live
3. "Export to Word when you're done. You can edit, regenerate any section, or chat with the builder to refine."

### Hero moment #4 — Pipeline overview (2 min)
1. Back to CRM
2. Show kanban view, toggle between list/kanban
3. Show filters (stage, industry, deal size, priority)
4. Show a risk flag on a deal
5. "Everything's multi-tenant and org-scoped — if you invite a co-investor, they only see what you share."

### Hero moment #5 — Collaboration (2 min)
1. Settings → Team → Invite Team Member
2. Show the role options (Member / Viewer / Admin)
3. "You get a shareable link immediately — email, Slack, whatever. No email delivery issues."

## 💰 When They Ask "How Much?"

**Never apologize. Never undersell.**

> "We're in private beta with a small group of firms right now. Pricing for production will be in the [X]-[Y] per seat per month range, but **beta users get 6 months free** in exchange for honest feedback and a testimonial we can use. Does that work for you?"

## 🏁 The Close (don't skip this)

**Never end with "let me know what you think."**

Instead:

> "Based on what you said about [their exact pain point from discovery], do you want to try this with a real CIM this week? I can send you an invite link right now and we do a 15-minute follow-up on Friday to look at how it went."

Then:
1. Send the invite link **while they watch** (Settings → Team → Invite)
2. Send the calendar link for Friday **while they watch** (your https://calendar.app.google/ link)
3. "Done. Invite is in your inbox. Looking forward to Friday."

## 🔥 Recovery Plays (when things break live)

| Problem | What to say + do |
|---|---|
| AI call takes >10 sec | "This is a cold start — we're on serverless, so the first call after idle takes a second. After that it's snappy." (Then show the backup recording while you wait) |
| 500 error | "Ha, beta software — this is exactly the kind of thing your feedback helps us catch. Let me show you the same flow on a different deal." (Switch to a pre-tested deal) |
| You forget what to say | Pause. Drink water. Ask "does that answer your question so far?" — buys 10 seconds |
| They ask a hard feature question | "Great question — let me write that down for our product roadmap. That's exactly the kind of input we need from beta users." |
| They're quiet/unengaged | Ask them a question: "Does this match how you think about deal review today?" |
| They push back on price | "Totally fair — the beta is free for 6 months so you can validate the ROI before any commitment." |

## 🧠 Demo Day Rules (tattoo these)

1. **They talk 60%, you talk 40%** (track it if you have to)
2. **Never demo a feature they didn't ask for** (even if you love it)
3. **Pause after wow moments** — 3 full seconds, let it sink in
4. **Talk slower than feels natural** — especially when nervous
5. **Take notes on screen** — they see you listening
6. **Never apologize for the product** — frame as "we're prioritizing X next"
7. **End with a concrete next step** — always
8. **Send the follow-up within 2 hours** — while they still remember you
