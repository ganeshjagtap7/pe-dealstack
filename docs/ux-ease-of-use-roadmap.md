# PE Dealstack — Ease-of-Use Roadmap

## 1. Diagnosis

Four root themes connect the 63 internal findings to why CRMs fail at small funds:

1. **The empty-CRM cold start.** Gmail — the product's core value driver — isn't offered at signup (`IntegrationsSection.tsx:17-23`), firm context is buried in Settings, and onboarding ends on dead surfaces. Affinity/Attio win precisely by making the first session show a populated pipeline ([Attio's data-first onboarding](https://strategybreakdowns.com/p/attio-shapeshifting-product)); ~2/3 of B2B signups never reach first value.
2. **Click-depth in the daily loop.** Stage changes are 4-5 clicks + a modal, every field edit is a modal round-trip, filters reset on refresh, zero keyboard shortcuts. This is the exact "too many clicks to one fact" complaint that kills DealCloud ([Capterra](https://www.capterra.com/p/139967/DealCloud/reviews/)) and the inline-edit/spreadsheet-feel gap vs Attio/folk.
3. **AI is built but invisible and untrusted.** The FAB, memo chat, per-deal chat, email summaries, and inbox scan all hide behind undiscoverable buttons, render uncited static text, and don't stream. AI value never lands as structured, glanceable data ([folk Magic Fields](https://help.folk.app/en/articles/7325564-magic-fields), [Attio AI attributes](https://attio.com/platform/ai)).
4. **Broken loops and dead ends.** Team invites that don't send, extraction failures with no recovery path, bulk imports with no drill-down, onboarding that strands partial progress. Each one teaches the user the tool can't be trusted — the spiral that drives them back to the spreadsheet.

Why: kills the "Select a folder first" blocked-upload error (`data-room/[dealId]/page.tsx:160-164`) and dedupes two findings. **Impact 4.**

Why: only 4 actions exist (`CommandPalette.types.ts:25-30`); Integrations is 6 clicks deep (`settings/page.tsx:23-33`); weak search is the #1 incumbent complaint ([G2 on Affinity](https://www.g2.com/products/affinity/reviews)). **Impact 4.**

## 3. Medium bets (2-5 days each)

1. **Gmail connect as an onboarding step, with a plain-language consent screen** — add a 4th checklist item with inline OAuth; consent screen states what's scanned and what's ignored. Why: Gmail is only discoverable in Settings (`IntegrationsSection.tsx:17-23`, `GmailSuggestions.tsx:194-201`); [4Degrees front-loads privacy controls](https://support.4degrees.ai/article/299-getting-started-with-4degrees-complete-guide) to convert OAuth hesitators; this is the gateway to every other auto-capture feature. **Impact 4.**
2. **Inline editing + one-gesture stage change on the pipeline** — click-to-edit deal name/industry/size (`edit-deal-modal.tsx`, `deal-page-header.tsx:127-134`), inline stage picker or drag on pipeline dots (`deal-layout.tsx:55-71`), optimistic updates. Why: the open-modal-save-go-back loop is "precisely the friction that makes people fall back to Excel" ([Attio teardown](https://strategybreakdowns.com/p/attio-shapeshifting-product)); dedupes 3 daily-loop findings. **Impact 4.**
3. **Editable extraction review before deal commit** — "Review & Fix" mode in `deal-intake/components.tsx:180-400` ResultDisplay: fields become inputs, × to discard low-confidence values, plus threshold guidance copy. Why: read-only results force post-creation rework on every weak extraction; "AI proposes, human confirms" is the trust-building pattern ([folk AI-native guide](https://www.folk.app/articles/ai-native-crm)). Dedupes the confidence-guidance finding. **Impact 4.**
4. **Stream the per-deal chat** — `POST /deals/:dealId/chat/stream` (SSE, same pattern as global chat in `apps/api/src/routes/ai.ts:141-200`); frontend in `AIAssistant.tsx:268-292` shows tokens + tool activity; add a "Try again" button on stream errors (`AIAssistant.tsx:341-374`). **Impact 4.**
5. **Cmd-K global deal/contact/doc search with verbs** — fuzzy search across deals (grouped by stage, recency-sorted) plus "ask AI about <deal>"; pin/favorite deals. Why: no global deal search today (`deals/page.tsx:300-338`); ship "one excellent cmd-K search before any reporting module" — the documented incumbent weak spot ([G2](https://www.g2.com/products/affinity/reviews)). **Impact 4.**
6. **"Last contact" / staleness columns on pipeline + contacts** — compute days-since-last-email per deal/contact from already-synced Gmail threads; "going cold" badge ≥N days. Why: [Affinity's non-deletable smart fields](https://support.affinity.co/s/article/Enriched-Global-and-List-specific-Fields) answer the partner's actual pipeline-review questions with zero typing; the documented gap of all incumbents. Fixes the generic `fmtNextAction` problem (`dashboard-widgets.tsx:97`) for free. **Impact 4.**
7. **"Create Investment Memo" from data-room insights** — CTA in `components/vdr/InsightsPanel.tsx` → `/memo-builder?dealId=X` with folder context; plus granular generate-all progress ("2 of 8 sections") via polled status (`memo-builder/page.tsx:289-299`). **Impact 4.**
8. **Structured citations in AI answers** — sources envelope from `globalChatAgent/index.ts:119` rendered as linked badges/collapsible Sources drawer in `deal-tabs-ai-message-body.tsx:31`; add "View Email" link on inbox-scan candidates (threadId already in `inbox-deals-modal.tsx:118-170`). Why: uncited AI figures are unverifiable; verifiability is what separates a trusted analyst from a toy. **Impact 3.**

## 4. Big bets (week+ each)

1. **Zero-data-entry first run: connect Gmail → the CRM builds itself.** On first OAuth, background-backfill 90 days of history: run the inbox deal scan (`apps/api/src/services/inboxDealScanService.ts`), auto-create contact/company records with signature parsing, and land the user on a pipeline pre-populated with their real deal flow in a "review these" tray. Why: this is [Affinity's day-one-value pitch](https://www.affinity.co/product/activity-capture) and [Attio's "3 weeks of setup in 3 minutes"](https://strategybreakdowns.com/p/attio-shapeshifting-product); the empty state is the #1 abandonment point and pe-dealstack already owns all the primitives. **Impact 5.**
2. **Unified "Suggested updates" queue (AI proposes, human confirms).** One review tray for: inbox deal candidates (replacing the orphaned `inbox-deals-modal.tsx` with bulk-create), stage-change suggestions inferred from email ("NDA signed → move to DD"), field updates from new docs (replacing silent auto-update at `documents-upload.ts:279-315`), and dormancy nudges with AI-drafted follow-ups. One-click accept, batch accept-all, audit trail. Why: converts "maintain the CRM" into "triage a short queue" — the only maintenance model a 1-person fund sustains ([adoption research](https://www.affinity.co/blog/crm-adoption-rates), [4Degrees decay automation](https://www.4degrees.ai/relationship-intelligence)); dedupes 4 findings across intake/docs-memo/ai-surfaces. **Impact 5.**
3. **Stage-aware PE pipeline + one-click tear sheet.** Ship the search-fund stage set (Sourced → NDA → CIM Review → IOI → LOI → DD → IC → Close) as the default; stage transitions surface the relevant existing feature (NDA → legal-doc send flow, DD → data room, IC → memo builder) with non-blocking readiness checklists ("EBITDA missing, no CIM uploaded") and a per-deal tear-sheet PDF export composed from extracted financials + teaser fit. Why: stage-native language is the one thing users praise incumbents for ([Meridian](https://www.meridian-ai.com/blog/best-private-equity-crm)); the tear sheet is PE's universal artifact ([V7](https://www.v7labs.com/blog/tear-sheet-generation)) and makes the AI extraction visibly shareable. **Impact 4.**
4. **AI columns on the deal list.** Expose existing agents (firm-fit teaser, financial extractor, next-step suggester) as sortable/filterable pipeline columns — "Fit score", "Seller motivation", "Suggested next step" — with per-cell regenerate and bulk-run, results persisted to deal fields not chat transcripts. Why: puts AI where decisions happen ([folk Magic Fields](https://help.folk.app/en/articles/7325564-magic-fields), [Attio research agent](https://attio.com/blog/introducing-attio-ai-research-agent)); sorting 50 inbound CIMs by fit score is the killer triage view, and the backend agents (`apps/api/src/services/agents/`) already exist. **Impact 4.**

## 5. Anti-patterns to never adopt

- **No vendor- or admin-gated configuration.** Every setting (stages, intake rules, teaser criteria) stays inline-editable with pre-seeded defaults; never a separate admin console requiring docs ([Capterra on DealCloud](https://www.capterra.com/p/139967/DealCloud/reviews/): "customization… needs to be done by a DealCloud agent").
- **No report builder.** Ship 2-3 opinionated, always-correct outputs (weekly digest, tear sheet, pipeline CSV) and let the chat agent answer ad-hoc questions; buggy configurable reporting destroys trust in the underlying data.
- **No generic custom objects or workflow builders.** Model everything as first-class PE concepts (Deal, Broker, CIM, NDA, Memo, Teaser) — Salesforce-on-PE inherits "very SQL-y, roundabout ways of doing things" ([4Degrees on Navatar](https://www.4degrees.ai/blog/top-dealcloud-alternatives)).
- **No hard-blocking stage gates.** Readiness checks nudge with one-click fixes; never prevent the drag. Rigid process enforcement is the most-hated DealCloud trait.
- **No silent AI mutation and no silently-dropped email.** Every AI write goes through the suggestion queue with provenance; inbox sync shows visible status ("last scanned 2 min ago") and a rescan affordance — flaky email trust killed incumbent deployments, and the existing Workspace account-routing glitch makes this doubly relevant.

## 6. Suggested sequencing (first 5, and why they compound)

1. **Gmail at onboarding + consent screen** (Medium #1) — the gateway. Nothing in the auto-capture story works for users who never connect; this single step raises the ceiling on every later item.
2. **Zero-data-entry first run / 90-day backfill** (Big #1) — builds directly on #1: the same OAuth now produces a populated pipeline, deleting the empty-state problem that the onboarding findings circle around. Instrument "first deal auto-detected" as the activation metric.
3. **Staleness / last-contact columns** (Medium #6) — the cheapest visible payoff of the synced data from #2: every pipeline open now proves the Gmail connection's value, and it feeds the dormancy logic in the queue.
4. **Inline editing + one-gesture stage change, plus the keyboard/palette quick-win cluster** (Medium #2 + Quick #1/#3/#6) — makes the now-populated pipeline pleasant to work in daily, replacing the spreadsheet rather than coexisting with it. Quick wins ship in parallel during weeks 1-2.
5. **Suggested-updates queue** (Big #2) — the compounding flywheel: #1-#3 generate signals (new threads, stage cues, going-cold deals), #4 makes acting instant, and the queue turns those signals into a sustainable daily triage habit — the difference between a CRM that's opened every morning and one that's abandoned at month two.
